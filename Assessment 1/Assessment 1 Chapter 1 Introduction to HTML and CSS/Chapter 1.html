Sinigang na Bangus Recipe

/*Using blockquote to indent the header 1*/

Sinigang na Bangus is a type of sour clear broth fish soup. This recipe that we have here is basic, but it is my favorite among all the versions that I have tried. My family likes this too because it is simple and delicious. You can really enjoy the fresh taste of the fish complemented by the warm sour broth.

/*Using the table tag to copy the format from my reference website*/
This is the estimated time for cooked Sinigang na Bangus
Prep: 10minutes minutes Cook: 30minutes minutes
Total: 40minutes minutes


/*br tag used for spacing

Ingredients

/*h2 = Header 2*/
    /*using unordered list for the ingredients as they don't need to be in order*/
  • ▢2 lbs. | 0.9kg bangus milkfish, cleaned and sliced /*li = list*/
  • ▢1 40g pack Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix (Original)
  • ▢1 bunch fresh kangkong leaves
    /*I added the nav tag here because the css applies to the list above and I don't know what I did wrong*/
  • ▢12 pieces sitaw snake beans, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • ▢6 to 8 pieces okra
  • ▢2 pieces long green pepper siling pansigang
  • ▢1 medium tomato wedged
  • ▢1 medium yellow onion wedged
  • ▢2 ½ tablespoons fish sauce
  • ▢¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ▢2 quarts water /*using strong, or b, and i tags for text emphasis*/

Instructions

    /*use of ordered list for the steps needed to follow*/
  1. Heat a cooking pot. Pour water into it.
  2. Add tomato and anion. Let boil.
  3. Add bangus. Cover and cook in medium heat between 8 to 12 minutes.
  4. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix. Stir. Cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Put the long green pepper into the pot. Add snake beans and okra. Stir. Cover and cook for 5 to 7 minutes.
  6. Add fish sauce and ground black pepper. Stir.
  7. Put the kangkong leaves into the pot. Cover the pot and turn the heat off. Let it stay for 5 minutes.
  8. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.
  9. Share and enjoy!
